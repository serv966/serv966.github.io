Microprocessor (U351)

The major functions of the microprocessor is to detect the front SW operation, to control each device, to control the OSD, to switch the frequency, to readout and restore the monitor adjustment data and to control the PowerSave. The microprocessor is always active while the monitor is powered on.

  1. SW Control
    The Key-Scan method is adopted at the switch control. The microprocessor detects what control SW is operated, with measuring the voltage on 95 pin (KY1).
    The microprocessor also controls the front LED on/off at the 4 pin (green) and 5 pin (yellow).
    -
  2. Data transmission
    The devices controlled by the microprocessor are EEPROM(U353), measure gate array(U352) , OSD(U205), Video pre amplifier (U202) DSP LSI(U601) and D/A converter (U551, U603, U570).
    These devices except measure gate array(U352) , OSD(U205) use I2C bus and transfer the data through SCL (29 pin) and SDA (30 pin).
    The measure gate array transmits data through the three-wire serial bus (DI: 77 pin, CK: 75 pin and CE: 76 pin). The OSD (U205) transmits data through the three-wire serial bus (DI: 31 pin, CK: 33 pin and OSD_CE: 84 pin).
    -
  3. AP1, AP2, PS
    AP1 (72 pin), AP2 (71 pin) and PS(5 pin) output from the microprocessor. control the power circuit.
    -
  4. Self-protecting function
    The microprocessor monitors whether or not the monitor's internal circuit works properly.
    The output lines that the microprocessor monitors are:
    * 170 V line
    * 80 V line
    * -14 V line
    * 5V_M line
    * Each Output voltage of speaker
    *Horizontal and Vertical oscillation line output by the H. BLK and VP_DET
    *X.pro voltage
    When any of these line stops outputting, the microprocessor finds any trouble that has occurred and stops the voltage except the 5V_S. The microprocessor leads the monitor's operation to the Power Save mode 2 to protect the monitor from further damage. Then, the microprocessor notifies the user of the trouble by flashing the front Yellow / Green LED. In addition, LED at the abnormalities operate by lighting with 8 cycle of the number of surveillance. Green blinking tells the unusual factor. This is useful when finding the defected part.
    To cancel the protector function, short between PC (9 pin) and GND (10 pin).
    LED blinking order Error location
    1 Speaker voltage
    2 5V_M
    3 -14V
    4 85V
    5 180V
    6 Horizontal (HFBP)
    7 Vertical (VPDET)
    8 Xpro voltage

    Yellow blinking fast WatchDog (Software protection)

    When protector functions are canceled, yellow horizontal lines appear on the OSD display. Red horizontal lines appear when the X-ray protector has not been adjusted. Also, a completely white screen is displayed in a no-signal state in the factory adjustment mode.
Measure gate array functions (U352)

The major functions of gate array are to measure the frequency of the input signal, Moiré canceling, to measure the input signal when Auto-Size is performed and PWM output.

  1. Measurement of the input signal frequency
    The gate array always measures the frequency of sync signal input both BNC and D-SUB. The measurement is sent to the microprocessor as data. The microprocessor reads the power saving information such as the existence of the input sync signal as the data.
    When Auto-Sizing is performed, the gate array measures the Display period and Back Porch of the input signal. And at the same time , it displays an image on the screen with proper size and position by measuring the H_BLK and VP DET. To decide the proper size, the gate array measures the Sync and R, G and B video of the input signal. From this measurement, the microprocessor calculates the Total period and Display period. Then, to decide the proper size, the microprocessor uses the data in a ratio of the Display period to the Total period. To decide the proper position, the gate array measures the wave which follows the H_BLK and VP DET.
    -
  2. Control Signal
    The U352 outputs the PWM to control the correction of Brightness, Cutoff, Moiré Cancel and the warm-up drift of the brightness.
EEPROM (U353)

The factory default data, user adjusted data and setting data for the microprocessor are stored within the U353. This data can be readout and restored with special utility software such as eep.exe. If you exchange the EEPROM, all of the adjusted data will be lost. So you need to save the adjusted data in the ROM to file before you exchange the EEPROM, or you need to readjust and store all of the adjustments after you have exchanged EEPROM.

When you exchange the EEPROM to a new one and turn on the Power SW for the first time, no power phenomenon will occur for approximately 6 sec. This is because the microprocessor formats the EEPROM, so the phenomenon does not indicate the monitor's trouble.

The major data stored in the EEPROM is:

adjusted data such as size, position, distortion, color and so on
data for the Auto-Size
switch setting of the CS-capacitor
limitation data for the scanning frequency
