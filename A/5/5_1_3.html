EMI Filter circuit
EMI Filter circuit has INLET-FILTER (IN001), NORMAL CHOKE (L005), LINE FILTER (L001, L002), BEADS (L003), X-CAPACITOR (C001) and Y-CAPACITOR (C090~C099) in order to meet the EMI/EMC regulation.
Degauss Circuit
At the degauss circuit, the damped oscillation current is made by running the input AC current through degaussing posistor. Then the CRT is degaussed by running this current to the degauss coil. The Degauss circuit works by receiving the control signal <ON (Hi: 5 V)/OFF (Lo: 0 V)> signal from the microprocessor. When the ON signal comes in from the microprocessor, the small-signal MOS-FET (Q971) turns ON and the relay (RY001) works. The RY001 flows the degauss current in the degauss coil.
The signal from the microprocessor turns ON for 4 seconds (Hi: 5V) after degaussing. After then, it keeps OFF (Lo: 0V) for 20 seconds in order to cool the degaussing posistor.
Inrush Current Prevention Circuit
The input smoothing capacitor (C010, C071) are charged when the power is on. These currents are called inrush current.
Because this excess inrush current may cause the circuit parts damge such as the welding of the contact point of the power SW and the fusion of the fuse, the inrush current prevention circuit is built in.
Firstly, the current flows into the C010 and C071 through the inruch current prevention resistor thermistor (TH001). At this time, the over-current cannot flow because the TH001 limits the current. The power circuit starts operating and then with a time lag the RY002 is ON. The current supplied through the TH001 comes to flow through the RY002.
Sub-converter Circuit

The sub-converter circuit has 6.3V of single output flyback converter, with insulated its primary and secondary line by the TRANS (T003).
At PowerSave mode , it works with the fixed oscillated frequency. At normal mode, it works with synchronizing to the sync. signal 2FH. The 6.3V voltage is used in the microprocessor, DSP block, the heater and USB block.

The principle of the operation
If the power SW turns ON, the Q031 turns ON. Then the operation current runs to the Vcc terminal (7pin) of the control IC: U071 for the sub-converter through the operation resistance R035~R037. Then the sub-converter starts working. Then, the Q031 turns OFF and the operation circuit stops. The tertiary winding output of T003 is changed to DC15V through the IC-REGULATOR and supplied to the Vcc of the U071.
The circuit charges the transformer (T003) with energy during the switch element (Q072) is on. And the circuit transfer the energy to the T003 secondary line from primary line and supplies the rectified and smoothed voltage through each output rectification diode (D907) and smoothing electrolytic capacitors (C911, C916) when the switch element is off.
This control varies ON duty of the switch element (PWM-Pulse Width Modulation) through the control IC (U071) in order to keep the output voltage (6.3V).
The output voltage is detected by the R908 and R909. It is input to the SHUNT-REGULATOR (U901: Reference voltage) and the FEED-BACK output is transmitted to the primary line by the photo coupler (PC003) and fed back to the control IC (U071 1 pin). The control IC works to keep the voltage feed back constant.
Main-converter Circuit

The main-converter circuit has many output flyback the dummy resonant converter, isolated its primary and secondary line with the TRANS (T001).

The principle of the operation
After the sub-converter is operated, the main-converter circuit receives the control signal (AP) from the microprocessor to start operating.
When the AP becomes high (5V), the Q902 is ON to flow the current in the photo coupler (PC004) and to transmit the high signal from the secondary line to the primary line. The signal turns the Q932 ON. The tertiary winding output 15V of the sub-converter is supplied to the Vcc terminal (4pin) of the control IC (U001) of the main-converter. Then the circuit operates. The IC has a main switching element and a sub switching element for the dummy resonant.
When the main switching element is on, the circuit charges energy in the transformer T001. And when the main switching element is off, the circuit release the energy to the secondary line of the transformer T001 and outputs rectified and smoothed voltage through rectification diode and smoothing electrolytic capacitor. The principle of the operation is same as before.
The output voltage control varies ON duty of the main switching element through the control IC (U001) in order to keep the output voltage of the 85V line.
The output voltage is detected by the R984, R985 and VR981. It is input to the SHUNT-REGULATOR (U981:IC for the reference voltage) The FEED-BACK output is transmitted to the primary line by the photo coupler (PC001) and fed back to the control IC (U001 7pin). The control IC works to keep the voltage fed back constant. The switching frequency synchronizes with 2fH.
At PowerSave Mode, the control signal AP from the microprocessor turns low level to stop operating the main-converter circuit.

Function of a Sub-Switching element
(Dummy Resonant Operation)
The sub switching element does the dummy resonant operation so that the main switching element can start in 0V in order to reduce the switching loss of the main switching element. The sub switching element turns ON 250nS earlier than the main switching element does to let the drain voltage of the main switching element resonate by the resonant capacitor (C021), the main transformer (T001) and the choking coil (L012). The dummy resonant operation starts when the main switching element turns 0V. Thus this function reduces the loss of the main switching element. In addition, although the main switching element is OFF, the main switching OFF wave stands up smoothly because the C021 is inserted to reduce the switching loss.

Countermeasure for higher harmonic distorted current
A countermeasure for higher harmonic distorted current is taken in the circuit consisted of PFC coil of maintrans (T001), D001-D013, D002-D003, C007, C008, and L004.
When the control IC(U001) is on and drain current of the main switching element runs in the Np coil, similar voltage to the drain voltage is produced in PFC coil.
This makes conduction angle (Charging current running term in capacitor) of C010, condenser of unstriated input, wider and improves its power factor.
As a result, the voltage between terminals of C010 becomes 10 to 20V higher than that in the capacitor and the input smoothing circuit. In addition, when the main switch is on, the similar current to drain current runs to PFC coil (L011). Then, the energy charged in L011 becomes swing back current when main switch is off and charges C010 through C013, C014, D004 and D005.
As a result, the conduction angle of C010 becomes wider and the power factor improves. 
Sync Signal Transmit Circuit
The sync signal is transmitted from the main block to the oscillation frequency of the power circuit with the operation frequency of the main block. When an input signal (fH) is higher than 63kHz, transmitted is the sync signal of the frequency which is the same as the input signal (fH). When the input signal (fH) is lower than 63kHz, transmitted is the sync signal of the frequency which is twice as high as the input signal.
The sync signal sent from the PCB-MAIN is used for the wave to drive Q941.
The Q941 drives the transformer (T002) for sync signal transmission to transmit the sync signal from the secondary line to the primary.
It is input to each control IC in the sub power circuit (U071) and the main-converter circuit (U001).
DC-DC Converter Circuit
The DC-DC converter has the output voltage variable flyback converter of TRANS (T951). This circuit receives the ERR (FEED-BACK voltage) and generates the +B voltage for the horizontal deflective circuit from the 85V line voltage of the main-converter.
The circuit charges the transformer (T951) with energy during the switch element (Q951) is on. And the circuit transfers the energy to the T951 secondary line from primary line and supplies the rectified and smoothed voltage through each output rectification diode and smoothing electrolytic capacitor when the switch element is off.
This control varies ON duty of the switch element (PWM) through the control IC (U951) so that the output voltage corresponds with ERR (FEED-BACK voltage) .
The output voltage is controlled by inputting the FEED-BACK voltage from the horizontal output circuit to the control IC (U951 2 pin).
The switching frequency is synchronizes with fH (horizontal oscillated frequency) by the sync. signal FBP.
 Protector relations

 
U001 excess voltage protector
When Vcc of U001, the voltage between end terminals of C022, surpasses 22V(TYP.),U001 stops its operation by protector.

U001 excess current protector
U001 stops its operation by protector when the voltage between end terminals of U001 current detector resistance is 0.88V(TYP.) for more than 1 seconds and stops its operation immediately when the voltage becomes over 1.5 V(TYP.).

U001 thermal protector
When U001 internal temperature reaches 125°C to 150°C, the protector works and stops the operation. 

Variable +B output excess voltage protector
This is a circuit which detects excess voltage of variable +B output (voltage at both ends of C961).
When the voltage divided by R971-R974 resistance exceeds Zener voltage 6.2, the thyristor circuit which consists of Q953 and Q954 turns on and connects 1pin of control IC (U951) to GND and stops the operation.
Then microcomputer protector, which detects horizontal output shutoff, operates and the product shifts to power saving mode.
The protector operates when C961 both edge voltage reaches around 200V.  

85V output excess voltage protector
This is a circuit which detects excess voltage of 85V line output.
When the divided voltage by R912-R914 resistance surpasses ZD902's Zener voltage 6.2V, the thyristor circuit which consists of Q903 and Q904 turns on and the anode side of photo coupler is connected to GND and Q043 turns off.
As a result, the voltage supply to U001 is cut off and U001 stops its operation.
Then, the output of the main converter is cut off and the microcomputer protector, which detects 85V, +14V and -14V, operates and the product shifts to power saving mode.
The protector operates when C961 both edge voltage reaches around 100V.
